About

I was born and raised in New York State. My parents are both artists. Our first family computer was a Tandy Sensation. I got a book on computer animation and was hooked. That was the path that led me to combining programming and art.

I left high school early to attend Bard College, where I studied computer graphics and got a well-rounded education in the humanities. After college I headed west, applying my coding skills to web and mobile development. I was a product designer at Electronic Arts, and in 2010 I created Polychord for iPad. I ended up at a small startup in Palo Alto called Timeful, working with some truly amazing people. In 2015 we were acquired by Google, where I spent eight years as a creative engineer.

I left Google to return to indie development. At Apposite, I'm building spatial apps like Vibescape for Apple Vision Pro and SageBrush, an AI painting app. On YouTube I make videos about spatial computing, tech history, and immersive worlds.

A few years ago, my wife and I began restoring a historic farm in the Pacific Northwest. We're raising our kids here, bringing back upland prairie and oak woodland on the property — two habitats that are rapidly disappearing. If that's the sort of thing that interests you, follow along on Instagram.
